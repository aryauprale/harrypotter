All About Harry Potter

What Is Harry Potter?


Harry Potter is a seven-book series written by British author J.K. Rowling.

The books follow the life and adventures of a young wizard, Harry Potter, as he discovers his magical heritage, attends the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, and faces the dark wizard Voldemort, who killed his parents and seeks to dominate the wizarding world.

Main Books in the Series:

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997)

Harry learns he's a wizard and begins attending Hogwarts. He uncovers the secret of the Philosopher's Stone and confronts Lord Voldemort for the first time.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (1998)

Harry returns to Hogwarts for his second year and investigates mysterious attacks on students, eventually discovering the Chamber of Secrets and defeating a manifestation of Voldemort.

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (1999)

Harry learns that a dangerous prisoner, Sirius Black, has escaped from Azkaban, but the truth behind Sirius’s past and his connection to Harry’s family is revealed.

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (2000)

Harry is unexpectedly entered into the Triwizard Tournament, a dangerous magical competition, where he faces life-threatening tasks and uncovers a plot by Voldemort to return to power.

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (2003)

Voldemort’s return is denied by the Ministry of Magic, and Harry forms “Dumbledore’s Army” to fight back. He also uncovers a secret prophecy about his connection to Voldemort.

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2005)

Harry learns more about Voldemort’s past and seeks out Horcruxes—magical objects that hold pieces of Voldemort’s soul. The book ends with a major loss.

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (2007)

The final showdown between Harry and Voldemort unfolds. Harry goes on a quest to destroy the Horcruxes and ultimately confronts Voldemort, leading to a climactic battle at Hogwarts.
