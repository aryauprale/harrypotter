Who I Am


Hello! My name is Arya Uprale and I am a passionate student with a huge love for reading.

I have been figuring out how to be a good student, balance work, and somehow fit in my other hobbies.

I thrive on creativity, connection, and making things happen —

whether that's through coding or just sharing a little bit of joy through volunteering.

I believe in reading being a caveat to living a healthier life,

and I'm always excited to learn, grow, and connect with like-minded people.

When I'm not coding, you can find me exercising or reading or volunteering.

Reading books has always been an important part of my life

because it has given me the knowledge to understand things in the world without having much experience in life.

There's so much to do in life and it's so hard to have a great work-life balance.

Lately, reading has been the last thing to do on my list and I don't want my life to be as such.

I used to never have problems with spelling things or writing essays because of how much I used to read.

I am on my journey to read much more and I hope some of you will try to read more as well.

I hope my website is a way for people to connect to their nostalgic past through remembering Harry Potter.

If you're interested in collaborating, chatting, or just want to connect,

don't be shy — I love meeting new people and exploring exciting opportunities!

  

What Harry Potter Is To Me


Harry Potter made up my entire childhood.

I was so enthralled with the wizarding world and following Harry Potter's fight against Voldemort.

Initially, he did not have much umderstanding of who Voldemort was.

As he grew older, he had to face his adversities and fears.

I really connected with how much more serious life became for him as Voldemort started to destroy the world.

Honestly, Harry Potter has been more than just a story to me;

it has been a journey of magic, friendship, and self-discovery.

Millions of people have always felt the same way about the series.

There may never again be a time where so many people read the same book

and could connect with each other and the wizarding world.

I remember growing up and talking to many people about all the books we had read.

Reading was a huge part of our lives and we all shared the many different emotions we felt while reading.

While reading Harry Potter I think I identified with the aspects of friendship and loyalty

within the story because they reflected my own relationships with others.

Overall, Harry Potter gave me a great sense of belonging

and taught me many moral lessons that gave me enough inspiration to overcome my own challenges.
